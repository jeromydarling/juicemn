VENDOR BOOTHS · 2026

Sell where the community actually is.

Three blocks of West Broadway. 5,000+ neighbors. Food sold out by 3 PM last year. Reserve a booth — food or non-food — and meet your next hundred customers face to face.

Why vendor

Real foot traffic. Real customers.

5,000+ neighbors

Minnesota's largest Juneteenth celebration. Proven foot traffic, families that arrive at 11 and stay until 7.

Built-in audience

People come to West Broadway hungry, ready to spend, looking to support Black-owned businesses. Food sold out by 3 PM last year.

Neighborhood market

No big-event corporate booth fees, no convention-center sterility. Just neighbors meeting neighbors on the block.

All-day energy

Parade, live music, carnival, car show, petting zoo — the crowd doesn't drift away. They eat, shop, come back.

Media attention

Local press, social-media amplification, community buzz. Your booth gets seen well beyond the day itself.

Mission aligned

JUICE — Juneteenth Urban Initiative Creating Economic-Empowerment — exists to lift up entrepreneurs. Your booth is the whole point.

Two booth options

Pick your block.

Both booths are $300, one-time, paid up front via Stripe. Reserve now — booths are limited and last year we ran out.

Food Vendor Booth
$300

One booth space for a food vendor. Bring your truck, cart, or tent — the neighborhood is hungry by noon. Includes recognition in the day-of program. At checkout we'll ask what you're serving and your electrical needs.

Non-Food Vendor Booth
$300

One booth space for goods, services, art, or advocacy. A great spot for Black-owned businesses, makers, and community organizations. At checkout we'll ask what you're selling and whether you need power.

What to know

The logistics.

  • Date: Friday, June 19, 2026 · 11 AM – 7 PM
  • Location: West Broadway Ave N, Minneapolis, MN 55411
  • Booth size: Standard 10×10 footprint. Food trucks and larger setups — just tell us at checkout.
  • What's included: Your booth space, recognition in the day-of program, all-day exposure to 5,000+ attendees.
  • What you bring: Your tent, table, chairs, signage, payment system (Square, etc.), and everything you're selling. Food vendors handle their own permits and food-handling certifications.
  • Power: Standard 110V is available — confirm at checkout. Heavy power needs (220V) and generators can be accommodated; we'll coordinate.
  • Load-in: Day-of starting at 8 AM. Specific load-in window confirmed by Todd a week before the event.
  • Refunds: Full refund up to 30 days before the event. After that, the booth fee supports event costs already committed.

After you pay, we'll send you a welcome email and reach out within 24 hours to confirm your details and answer any questions.

Questions

Not sure if your business fits? Just call.

We'd rather have a 5-minute conversation than have you wonder. If your booth idea doesn't cleanly fit "food" or "non-food," or if you have power needs we should plan around, or if you want to bring a bigger setup than a 10×10 — pick up the phone.
